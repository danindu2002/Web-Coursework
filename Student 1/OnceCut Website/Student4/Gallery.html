Action:

Fast X

Director:

Louis Leterrier

In its third decade and still going strong with the same core cast and characters as when it started, Fast X, the tenth installment in the Fast and Furious Saga, ushers in the closing chapters of one of cinema's most legendary and well-liked global series. Dom Toretto (Vin Diesel) and his family have outsmarted, out-nerved, and out-driven every adversary in their way over numerous missions and against insurmountable odds. They now face the deadliest foe they've ever encountered.

Mission Impossible (Rogue Nation)

Director:

Christopher McQuarrie

The fifth film in the Mission: Impossible film franchise. Tom Cruise plays the lead role.

Hunley of the CIA persuades a Senate committee to dissolve the IMF (Impossible Mission Force), of which Ethan Hunt ( Tom Cruise) is a major member. Hunley believes the IMF is too risky. Now on his own, Hunt pursues the Syndicate, a shady and deadly renegade organization.

Top Gun (Maverick)

Director:

Joseph Kosinski

Top Gun: Maverick is an American action drama film released in 2022 featuring Tom Cruise as naval aviator Maverick.

It follows Maverick's return to the United States Navy Strike Fighter Tactics Instructor program (also known as U.S. Navy-Fighter Weapons School - "TOPGUN"), where he must confront his past while training a group of younger pilots, including the son of Maverick's deceased best friend Lieutenant Nick "Goose" Bradshaw.

Deadpool

Director:

Tim Miller

Deadpool is a 2016 American superhero film based on the same-named Marvel Comics character. It is a spin-off in the X-Men film franchise, distributed by 20th Century Fox.

This is the origin narrative of Wade Wilson, a former Special Forces operator turned mercenary who adopts the alter name Deadpool after being subjected to a rogue experiment that leaves him with fast healing powers. Deadpool seeks after the man who nearly destroyed his life, armed with his new talents and a nasty, twisted sense of humour.

Sci-Fi:

Interstellar

Director:

Christopher Nolan

The epic science fiction film Interstellar was released in 2014.Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Chastain, Bill Irwin, Ellen Burstyn, Matt Damon, and Michael Caine feature in the film.

Disasters, famines, and droughts have plagued Earth's future. Interstellar travel is the only method to assure humanity's survival. A newly found wormhole in our solar system permits a team of astronauts to go to a planet where no one has gone before, a planet that may have the perfect climate to sustain human life.

Arrival

Director:

Denis Villeneuve

Arrival is a science fiction drama film released in 2016.Amy Adams plays Louise Banks in the film.

When massive spaceships crash-land at 12 different locations around the planet, linguistics professor Louise Banks leads an elite team of investigators. As the world prepares for war, Banks and her crew must race against the clock to find a way to communicate with the extraterrestrial visitors. In order to solve the enigma, she takes a risk that could endanger her life and possibly the entire human race.

The Martian

Director:

Ridley Scott

The Martian is a 2015 science fiction film and starring Matt Damon.

Astronaut Mark Watney is assumed dead after being left behind by his crew during a manned mission to Mars. Watney, on the other hand, has survived and is now stranded and alone on the inhospitable planet. With few resources, he must rely on his resourcefulness, intelligence, and spirit to survive and find a way to communicate with Earth. NASA and a multinational team of scientists work feverishly to bring "the Martian" home, while his crewmates plan a daring, if not impossible, rescue attempt. As these extraordinary stories of bravery surface, the globe comes together to pray for Watney's safe return.

Chappie

Director:

Neill Blomkamp

Chappie is a 2015 American dystopian science fiction action film.

Tetravaal Company's robots are used by the police department in Johannesburg to lower crime rates. Deon Wilson, an engineer who created Moose, is jealous of Deon's AI endeavor. Chappie, a wounded robot, is kidnapped by Deon and made to rob banks. Chappie is requested by the crooks Ninja, Yo-Landi, and America to halt the robot police. Chappie needs instruction to develop, as Vincent hatches a cunning plan to turn on his robot.

Fantasy:

Lord Of The Rings 1

Director:

Peter Jackson

Based on the 1954 book The Fellowship of the Ring, the epic fantasy adventure movie The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring was released in 2001.

Gandalf has located a lost Ring, the One Ring of the Dark Lord Sauron. To destroy the Ring, Frodo must set out on an epic journey with Gandalf, Legolas, Gimli, Aragorn, Boromir, and his friends Merry, Pippin, and Samwise, while overcoming several difficulties and perils. The Dark Lords' rule can only be ended if they succeed in their mission.

Avatar

Director:

James Cameron

Avatar is a epic science fiction film starring Sam Worthington, Zoe Saldana, Stephen Lang, Michelle Rodriguez, and Sigourney Weaver.

Jake Sully undertakes a mission on Pandora, where he discovers Parker Selfridge's avaricious plans to mine for the Na'vi, a local humanoid. Jake learns about the Indigenous Race and culture for a military force lead by Colonel Quaritch in exchange for spinal surgery. Using a "avatar" identity, he infiltrates the Na'vi. Jake develops a bond with the tribe and falls in love with Neytiri, but the Colonel's extermination techniques push Jake to take a stand and fight for Pandora's survival.

Harry Potter(the Goblet of Fire)

Director:

Mike Newell

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire is a fantasy film released in 2005.J.K. Rowling's 2000 novel of the same name inspired the film.Daniel Radcliffe plays Harry Potter in the film.

Harry begins his fourth year at Hogwarts with a summer break with friends. After the Quidditch World Cup Final, persons dressed as Lord Voldemort's "Death Eaters" set fire to tents and Voldemort's emblem, the "Dark Mark," sparking mayhem in the magical world. The "Triwizard Tournament," a magical tournament between Hogwarts, Beauxbatons, and Durmstrang schools, is held at Hogwarts. The candidates are chosen by the Goblet of Fire, a magical artefact, but Harry is chosen as the Fourth Champion. Harry, unaware of the magic's potency, must complete three difficult tasks.

Jumanji (Welcome to the Jungle)

Director:

Jake Kasdan

The 2017 American fantasy adventure comedy film Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle.Dwayne Johnson, Jack Black, Kevin Hart, Karen Gillan, and Nick Jonas star.

In Jumanji, four high school students find an outdated video game system and transform into adult avatars. To escape and return to the real world, they must survive the game, uncover Alan Parrish's legacy, and modify their self-perception. They must adjust their thinking or they will be locked in the game, playing by others for the rest of their lives.

Animation:

Minions

Director:

Pierre Coffin

Minions is a 2015 computer-animated comedy film released in the United States.Illumination Entertainment produced the film, which was released by Universal Pictures.

The Minions have existed from the beginning of time to serve the most loathsome of lords. The easily distracted tribe has aided the largest and worst of baddies, from the T-Rex to Napoleon. Join Kevin, the protective leader, Stuart, the young rebel, and endearing little Bob on a global road trip. They'll win the opportunity to work for a new boss, the world's first female supervillain, and attempt to preserve Minionkind from extinction.

How to Train Your Dragon 2

Director:

Dean DeBlois

How to Train Your Dragon 2 is a 2014 American computer-animated action fantasy film largely based on the book series of the same name.

Hiccup and Toothless embark on a voyage through the skies five years after reconciling dragons and vikings on Berk Island, discovering new planets and competing in dragon races. They uncover a secret ice cave inhabited by hundreds of wild dragons and the enigmatic Dragon Rider, sparking a battle to preserve peace. Hiccup and Toothless must band together to fight for their values and change the fates of men and dragons.

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Director:

Bob Persichetti

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is a computer-animated superhero film released in 2018. Spider-Man from Marvel Comics is shown.It was the Spider-Man franchise's first animated film.

The creative brains behind The Lego Movie and 21 Jump Street, Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, bring their distinctive talents to a fresh vision of a reimagined Spider-Man Universe, with a pioneering visual style that is the first of its kind. "Spider-Man(TM): Into the Spider-Verse" introduces Brooklyn youngster Miles Morales (Shameik Moore) and the Spider-Verse's boundless possibilities, where more than one person can wear the mask.

The Croods

Director:

Kirk DeMicco

DreamWorks Animation's 2013 computer-animated adventure comedy picture The Croods is distributed by 20th Century Fox.

The Croods are an odd family of cavemen that survive the tough terrain by following a rigid set of rules. When their home is destroyed in the aftermath of an imminent tragedy known as "The End," they are forced to flee into the wilderness of the unknown in search of a new home.
